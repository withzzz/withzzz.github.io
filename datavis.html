Word Sphere

_

(visualization of Mobydick)

 

I’m always interested in creating a space that could give people a whole picture about the data, and at the same time allow people to explore more.

Here’s a Word Sphere that shows the word-count in an article. We can easily have a general idea about the length of the article, the richness of the word pool and the word style of the author.

I take Shakespeare’s works and T.S.Eliot's works as an example. Shakespeare's work will repeat certain words more often. Especially in the Sonnets. While Eliot will use different rebundant words:

People could also zoom in and out to explore the details of the sphere. They can see what exactly the most common used words are.




Great Depressions in the USA

_

A visualization of the mentions of the word “depression” in NYTimes from 1900 to 2014. This work aims at exploring the relationship between data and aesthetics. It reveals This graphic purposefully sacrifice clarity in narrative for visual design, in order to let people focus on the great difference between the Great Depression and other years.

I built a 3D model that could allow people to explore, with drawing a diagram every year based on every month’s data, and arrange them side by side.

Static data graph for Jer Thorp’s Data Art at ITP. Created by Processing, the New York Times API.
